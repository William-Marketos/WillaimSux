The Fox Who Knew Too Much

Deep in the amber woods, where the leaves never quite decided between gold and red, there lived a fox named Ember. She was not the fastest fox, nor the largest — but she was, without question, the most curious.

Every morning she would trot to the edge of the meadow and sit very still, watching the world arrange itself. She memorised the paths of the bees, the arguments of the crows, and the exact moment the light turned silver before a storm.

One autumn evening, a lost traveller stumbled into the woods and sat down against an oak, defeated. Ember crept close — closer than foxes usually dare — and simply sat beside him. She didn't speak, of course. She was a fox. But she stayed until his breathing slowed and his shoulders dropped.

In the morning, the traveller found the path home without knowing quite why he felt so certain of the way. And Ember trotted back into the trees, already watching, already learning the next small thing the world had hidden in plain sight.
